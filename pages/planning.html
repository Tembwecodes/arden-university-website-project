Visa Requirements

Visa-Free Countries (90 days)

Citizens from these regions can enter South Africa without a visa for tourism:

  • European Union countries
  • United Kingdom
  • United States
  • Canada
  • Australia
  • New Zealand

Required Documents:

  • Passport valid for at least 30 days after departure
  • Two blank pages for entry/exit stamps
  • Proof of sufficient funds
  • Return ticket or onward travel proof

Health & Safety Guidelines

General Safety

  • Stay aware of your surroundings
  • Keep valuables secure and out of sight
  • Use reputable tour operators
  • Travel in groups when possible
  • Keep copies of important documents

Wildlife Safety

  • Always follow park rules and guide instructions
  • Maintain safe distance from all animals
  • Never feed wildlife
  • Stay in vehicles during game drives
  • Be aware of venomous snakes and spiders

Best Time to Visit

Dry Season (May - September)

Best for: Wildlife viewing and outdoor activities

  • Animals concentrate around water sources
  • Cooler temperatures
  • Lower malaria risk

Wet Season (October - April)

Best for: Bird watching and budget travel

  • Lush, green landscapes
  • Baby animals and calving season
  • Lower accommodation rates

Local Customs & Cultural Etiquette

Greetings

Handshakes are common. Use both hands when greeting elders as a sign of respect. "Sawubona" (Zulu) and "Molo" (Xhosa) are common greetings.

Tipping Practices

Restaurants: 10-15% of bill. Safari guides: R50-100 per day. Hotel porters: R10-20 per bag. Tipping is customary and appreciated.

Dining Etiquette

Wait to be seated at restaurants. Keep hands visible on the table. Try local dishes like bobotie, biltong, and bunny chow.

Cultural Sensitivity

Ask permission before photographing people. Respect sacred sites. Learn basic phrases in local languages. Be mindful of South Africa's diverse heritage.
